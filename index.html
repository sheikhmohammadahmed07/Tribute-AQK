Abdul Qadeer Khan

Abdul Qadeer Khan, a Pakistani nuclear physicist and metallurgical engineer, is best known for his role in Pakistan's atomic bomb program. Born on April 1, 1936, in Bhopal, British India, Khan migrated to Pakistan after the partition.

Early life and education

Khan completed his early education in Bhopal and Karachi before moving to Europe for higher studies. He earned a degree in metallurgical engineering from the Technical University of Berlin, followed by advanced studies in Delft, Netherlands, and a Ph.D. from the Catholic University of Leuven in Belgium.

Career in Europe

Khan worked in Europe, gaining valuable experience in the nuclear field. His tenure at the Physical Dynamics Research Laboratory in the Netherlands was particularly significant, where he had access to advanced centrifuge technology.

Return to Pakistan

In 1975, Khan returned to Pakistan, bringing with him crucial knowledge and contacts. He played a pivotal role in establishing Pakistan's uranium enrichment program at Kahuta, which ultimately led to the development of nuclear weapons.

Contributions to Pakistan's Nuclear Program

Khan's efforts were instrumental in Pakistan conducting its first nuclear tests in 1998. He is celebrated as a national hero for ensuring Pakistan's security and maintaining a balance of power in the region.

Later Life

He remained a prominent figure in Pakistan, receiving numerous honors and continuing to contribute to science and education until his death on October 10, 2021.
